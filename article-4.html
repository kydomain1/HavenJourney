Hidden Gems: 10 Underrated Destinations for Summer 2025

Michael Thompson Travel Editor
June 22, 2025 12 min read 42 comments

While everyone's flocking to the same overcrowded hotspots, savvy travelers are discovering breathtaking destinations that offer authentic experiences, stunning landscapes, and memorable moments without the tourist hordes.

Summer 2025 presents a unique opportunity to explore places that have flown under the radar for too long. These destinations offer everything you'd want from a perfect getaway—natural beauty, rich culture, exceptional hospitality—without the inflated prices and overwhelming crowds that plague more famous locations.

Why Choose Underrated Destinations?

Overtourism has become a serious problem in many popular destinations, degrading both the visitor experience and local communities. By choosing lesser-known locations, you're not only guaranteeing yourself a more authentic experience but also supporting local economies that genuinely benefit from tourism without being overwhelmed by it.

Venturing off the beaten path reveals landscapes untouched by mass tourism.

These hidden gems often offer better value for money, with high-quality accommodations and dining experiences at a fraction of the cost you'd pay in tourist meccas. More importantly, you'll have room to breathe, opportunities for genuine cultural exchange, and the chance to discover places before they become the next big thing.

Our Top Picks for Summer 2025

1. Faroe Islands, Denmark

Dramatic cliffs, cascading waterfalls, and villages that look like they're straight out of a fantasy novel—the Faroe Islands offer some of Europe's most spectacular landscapes with only a fraction of Iceland's tourist traffic. The midnight sun in summer creates magical golden hours that photographers dream about.

The Faroe Islands combine Viking heritage with pristine Nordic wilderness.

2. Puglia, Italy

While tourists crowd Tuscany and the Amalfi Coast, Puglia offers equally stunning coastal scenery, exceptional cuisine, and charming whitewashed towns—all at significantly lower prices. The trulli houses of Alberobello and the baroque architecture of Lecce provide unforgettable photo opportunities.

3. Slovenia

This small Alpine nation punches well above its weight in natural beauty. From the fairytale Lake Bled to the mysterious Postojna Cave and the charming capital Ljubljana, Slovenia offers diverse experiences in a compact, easy-to-navigate package.

4. Albania

The Albanian Riviera rivals Greece's islands for beauty but costs a fraction of the price. Crystal-clear waters, ancient ruins, Ottoman architecture, and some of Europe's friendliest locals make Albania an incredible value destination. The mountain town of Gjirokastër, a UNESCO World Heritage site, transports you back centuries.

5. Colombia (Beyond Cartagena)

Colombia's diverse landscapes range from Caribbean beaches to Andean peaks.

While Cartagena gets all the attention, destinations like Salento in the coffee region, the colonial town of Villa de Leyva, and the beaches of Palomino offer equally compelling experiences. Colombia's transformation into a safe, welcoming destination is one of travel's best-kept secrets.

Travel Planning Tips for Hidden Gems

  • Research Infrastructure: Lesser-known destinations may have limited tourist facilities—plan accordingly
  • Learn Basic Phrases: English may be less common; a few local phrases go a long way
  • Book Accommodations Early: Limited options mean planning ahead is essential
  • Respect Local Culture: These destinations aren't tourism-hardened; be extra mindful of customs
  • Consider Shoulder Season: June and September often offer better weather than peak July/August
  • Travel Insurance: Always essential, especially in less-developed destinations

6. Azores, Portugal

This volcanic archipelago in the mid-Atlantic offers dramatic landscapes, incredible whale watching, hot springs, and hiking opportunities that rival Iceland or New Zealand—without the crowds or costs. Each of the nine islands has its own character, from the green crater lakes of São Miguel to the volcanic wine culture of Pico.

7. Georgia (The Country)

Ancient wine traditions, stunning mountain scenery, a unique alphabet, and cuisine that will make you reconsider everything you thought you knew about food. Georgia offers incredible hospitality, with locals often inviting visitors to join family feasts. The country sits at the crossroads of Europe and Asia, creating a fascinating cultural blend.

8. Northern Vietnam

While most tourists stick to Hanoi and Ha Long Bay, northern regions like Ha Giang and Sapa offer some of Southeast Asia's most spectacular landscapes. Terraced rice fields, ethnic minority villages, and winding mountain roads create unforgettable experiences.

9. Tasmania, Australia

Tasmania's pristine wilderness and farm-to-table dining scene rival anywhere in the world.

Australia's often-overlooked island state punches well above its weight with pristine wilderness, world-class art and food scenes, and landscapes that range from white sand beaches to alpine highlands. The capital Hobart has transformed into a cultural powerhouse, while places like Freycinet National Park offer wilderness experiences that feel truly remote.

10. Uruguay

South America's smallest Spanish-speaking country offers sophisticated wine regions, charming colonial towns, gorgeous beaches, and a relaxed lifestyle that makes extended stays tempting. The capital Montevideo blends European elegance with South American warmth, while Punta del Este offers upscale beach living without Brazilian prices.

Sustainable Travel Considerations

When visiting these emerging destinations, we have a responsibility to travel sustainably. Choose local accommodations over international chains, eat at family-owned restaurants, hire local guides, and respect environmental regulations. Your choices today determine whether these places remain hidden gems or become the next overtourished hotspots.

The Adventure Awaits

Summer 2025 offers the perfect opportunity to step off the beaten path and discover destinations that offer authentic experiences, stunning beauty, and genuine cultural immersion. These hidden gems won't stay hidden forever—visit now, travel responsibly, and create memories in places where tourism still feels like a privilege rather than an industry.
